Star Wars Jedi

The Story of
Cal Kestis

Order 66 has fallen. The Jedi are hunted. But one Padawan survived — and his journey will change the galaxy forever.

A Survivor's Path

Set five years after the events of Star Wars: Revenge of the Sith, Jedi: Fallen Order follows Cal Kestis — a young Padawan who survived Order 66 by hiding his Force abilities and working as a scrapper on the planet Bracca.

When his powers are accidentally revealed, Cal becomes the target of the Empire's Inquisitors, elite Force-sensitive hunters tasked with eliminating the last of the Jedi. Forced to run, Cal teams up with former Jedi Knight Cere Junda and pilot Greez Dritus aboard the ship Stinger Mantis.

"I'm no Jedi — not yet. But I will be." — Cal Kestis

The Mission

Cal's goal: find a Holocron — an ancient Jedi artifact containing a list of every Force-sensitive child in the galaxy. In the right hands, it could be the key to rebuilding the Jedi Order. In the wrong hands, it would hand the Empire the power to eliminate any future threat forever.

The journey takes Cal across stunning alien worlds — from the industrial ruins of Bracca to the ancient forests of Kashyyyk, the windswept cliffs of Zeffo, the depths of Dathomir, and beyond.

Lightsaber Combat

A precise, skill-based combat system inspired by challenging action games. Block, parry, and riposte with perfect timing, or Force Push enemies off cliffs. No button mashing — mastery is earned.

🌀

Force Powers

Slow time, push, pull, and unleash the Force in creative ways. Unlock new abilities as you progress through the story and revisit earlier areas with newly learned skills.

🗺️

Metroidvania Exploration

Each planet is an interconnected map full of optional paths, hidden collectibles, and secrets. New Force abilities and tools unlock previously inaccessible routes, rewarding thorough exploration.

🧩

Environmental Puzzles

Use the Force and BD-1's tools to solve puzzles woven into the environment. Ancient Zeffo tombs hide challenges that test your creativity and lateral thinking.

🎨

Customization

Personalize Cal's lightsaber, outfit, and BD-1's appearance with cosmetics discovered through exploration. Hundreds of combinations make your Jedi uniquely yours.

🏆

Skill Tree

Spend skill points earned in combat to unlock new moves, Force abilities, and passive upgrades. Build Cal's fighting style your way — aggressive attacker or patient defender.

Latest Updates

News &
Articles

15.11.2019
Release Day — A New Hope for Star Wars Gaming

Star Wars Jedi: Fallen Order launched on November 15, 2019, to widespread critical acclaim. Developed by Respawn Entertainment — the studio behind Titanfall and Apex Legends — the game marked EA's first major single-player Star Wars title in over a decade. Critics and fans alike hailed it as a return to form for the franchise, citing its tight combat, compelling story, and rich world-building. The game sold over 10 million copies in its first year.

29.10.2019
The First Story-Driven Title in a Decade

Jedi: Fallen Order represented a seismic shift in EA's approach to Star Wars games. Following years of controversy over multiplayer-focused titles and microtransactions, Respawn's single-player, narrative-driven adventure was exactly what fans had been asking for. Set between Episode III and Episode IV, the game explores a critical gap in the Star Wars timeline and introduces an entirely original cast of characters.

9.06.2018
First Announcement at EA Play 2018

EA and Respawn Entertainment officially announced Star Wars Jedi: Fallen Order at EA Play in June 2018, confirming that a single-player, narrative-driven Star Wars game was in development. The announcement was met with enormous excitement from the community. Lead writer Aaron Contreras and game director Stig Asmussen teased an action-adventure title drawing inspiration from challenging combat games while staying deeply rooted in Star Wars lore.

About the Developer

Respawn
Entertainment

The studio that brought you Titanfall, Apex Legends, and the finest Star Wars game ever made.

About Respawn

Founded in 2010 by Vince Zampella and Jason West — the original creators of Call of Duty — Respawn Entertainment has established itself as one of the most innovative studios in the industry. Based in Los Angeles, California, Respawn is known for creating games with a distinct feel for movement, momentum, and momentum-based combat.

After the breakthrough success of Titanfall and the phenomenon that became Apex Legends, Respawn took on the challenge of bringing a single-player, story-driven Star Wars experience to life — and delivered Jedi: Fallen Order, which sold over 10 million copies in its first year.

🎮

Titanfall (2014)

Respawn's debut — a fast-paced multiplayer shooter that introduced wall-running, mechs, and momentum-based movement to a wide audience.

🏆

Apex Legends (2019)

A genre-defining battle royale that accumulated over 70 million players in its first year and is still going strong today.

⭐

Jedi: Fallen Order (2019)

The studio's first single-player narrative adventure — and possibly their most acclaimed game to date, beloved by critics and fans worldwide.

"We wanted to make a game that felt authentically Star Wars — not just skin-deep, but in every mechanic and every moment." — Stig Asmussen, Game Director

Lore & Canon

The Star Wars
Universe

How Jedi: Fallen Order fits into the larger Star Wars galaxy — and why it matters.

Canon Placement

Jedi: Fallen Order is set in 14 BBY (Before the Battle of Yavin) — five years after the events of Star Wars: Episode III – Revenge of the Sith, and seventeen years before Episode IV – A New Hope. This places it squarely in the Imperial Era, one of the darkest periods in the galaxy's history.

The Dark Times. The period between Episodes III and IV, when the Emperor's grip on the galaxy tightened and the last Jedi went into hiding — or were hunted down.
🌌

The Galactic Empire

Emperor Palpatine rules with an iron fist. Clone troopers have been replaced by Stormtroopers. Dissent is crushed. The Jedi are extinct — or so the Empire believes.

🔴

The Inquisitors

Dark side warriors tasked with hunting down surviving Jedi. Former Padawans and Knights broken by the Empire and turned against their own kind — a chilling concept that first appeared in comics.

🌿

The Wookiees

On Kashyyyk, the Wookiees — Chewbacca's species — are enslaved by the Empire. Jedi: Fallen Order is one of the few stories to explore this important thread from the wider Star Wars canon.

Connected Canon

Fallen Order connects to a broader network of Star Wars stories. The Nightsisters of Dathomir were first explored in Star Wars: The Clone Wars. The Purge Troopers and Inquisitors appear in both comics and the Obi-Wan Kenobi Disney+ series. BD-1's discovery of the fallen Jedi Cordova ties into lore threads extending far beyond the game itself.

Common Issues

The game won't launch on PC
Try verifying game files through your platform client (Steam, EA App, or Epic). Ensure your GPU drivers are up to date. If using an NVIDIA card, check that DirectX 11 is enabled.
Progress not saving
Fallen Order autosaves at meditation points. Make sure you're resting at a meditation point before quitting. Cloud save sync issues can sometimes cause lost progress — check your platform's cloud save settings.
Cal is stuck and can't move
If Cal becomes stuck in geometry, open the menu and select "Return to Last Meditation Point." This respawns you safely without losing progress.
